Community Fireworks Mark 25 Years as America Turns 250

The Deland Rd Fireworks return for a milestone Fourth of July celebration honoring a quarter-century local tradition and the 250th Birthday of the United States.

This year’s Deland Rd Fireworks celebration carries extra meaning. Families, friends, and neighbors will gather for the 25th Anniversary of the Deland Rd fireworks tradition while also joining communities across the country in celebrating America’s 250th Birthday.

The event brings together the best parts of the Fourth of July: red, white, and blue pride, family time, food, handmade patriotic bracelets, and a larger community fireworks show from both Deland Rd South and Deland Rd North.

A local tradition. A national milestone. One big patriotic night.

Old Dad’s BBQ will begin serving at 3:00 PM from the viewing area at the back of the property facing west. Kid-friendly options will also be available, including hot dogs, chips, juice boxes, and slushies.

Before the real show begins, visitors can open the interactive launch page to tap the sky, play the announcement, and fire off digital fireworks. It is festive, harmless, and less likely to alarm the neighbors’ dog than the real thing. Progress, allegedly.
